No step needs a second hand

Most "remote mouse" apps assume two steady hands somewhere in the flow. This one never does — including setup.

Every action is one finger

Pointer, click, right-click, scroll, type, launch an app, switch windows — each one is a single-finger gesture or a single tap. There is no "now hold these two keys" moment, anywhere.

Large, forgiving targets

Controls are sized for a thumb, not a pixel hunt. If your grip, reach or precision varies day to day, the interface doesn't punish you for it.

Type at your own pace

The composer keyboard lets you write and edit the whole message on your iPhone first, then insert it on the Mac in one go. No racing a live cursor in someone else's text field.

Two-handed chords become taps

Keyboard shortcuts that normally need both hands stretched across a keyboard — copy, paste, switch apps, take a screenshot — fire from a single tap on your phone.

One-handed end to end — setup included

Pairing is scanning a QR code on your Mac's screen. No typing codes, no entering IP addresses, no settings spelunking.

Install the free Mac app

A small menu-bar companion. Open it once, approve the standard macOS permissions, and it shows a QR code.

Point your iPhone at it

The camera is used only to read that code — scan it with the phone in whichever hand is free.
